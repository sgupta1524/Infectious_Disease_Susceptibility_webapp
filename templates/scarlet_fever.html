     Scarlet fever
    Overview
	Scarlet fever is a bacterial illness that develops in some people who have strep throat. Also known as scarlatina, scarlet
	fever features a bright red rash that covers most of the body. Scarlet fever is almost always accompanied by a sore throat
	and a high fever.
    Symptoms
	Red rash, red lines, flushed face, strawberry tongue.
    Causes
	Scarlet fever is caused by the same type of bacteria that cause strep throat. In scarlet fever, the bacteria release a
	toxin that produces the rash and red tongue.
    Risk factors
	Children 5 to 15 years of age are more likely than are other people to get scarlet fever. Scarlet fever germs spread
	more easily among people in close contact, such as family members or classmates.
