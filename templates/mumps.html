     Mumps
    Overview
	Mumps is a viral infection that primarily affects saliva-producing (salivary) glands that are located near your ears.
	Mumps can cause swelling in one or both of these glands.
    Symptoms
	Some people infected with the mumps virus have either no signs or symptoms or very mild ones. When signs and symptoms
	do develop, they usually appear about two to three weeks after exposure to the virus.
    Causes
	Mumps is caused by a virus that spreads easily from person to person through infected saliva. If you're not immune,
	you can contract mumps by breathing in saliva droplets from an infected person who has just sneezed or coughed.
	You can also contract mumps from sharing utensils or cups with someone who has mumps.
    Prevention
	The best way to prevent mumps is to be vaccinated against the disease. Most people have immunity to mumps once
	they're fully vaccinated.
