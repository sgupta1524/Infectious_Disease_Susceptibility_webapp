     Intestinal parasites
    Overview
	Giardia infection is an intestinal infection marked by stomach cramps, bloating, nausea and bouts of watery diarrhea. Giardia
	infection is caused by a microscopic parasite that is found worldwide, especially in areas with poor sanitation and unsafe water.
    Symptoms
	Watery, sometimes foul-smelling diarrhea that may alternate with soft, greasy stools, fatigue, stomach cramps and bloating, gas,
	nausea, and weight loss
    Causes
	Giardia parasites live in the intestines of people and animals. Before the microscopic parasites are passed in stool, they become
	encased within hard shells called cysts, which allows them to survive outside the intestines for months. Once inside a host, the
	cysts dissolve and the parasites are released.
    Risk factors
	Children, people without access to clean drinking water, and men who have sex with men.
