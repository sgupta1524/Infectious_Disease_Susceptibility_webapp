     Chronic sinus infection
    Overview
	Chronic sinusitis occurs when the spaces inside your nose and head (sinuses) are swollen and inflamed for three months
	or longer, despite treatment.
    Symptoms
	Nasal inflammation, Thick, discolored discharge from the nose, drainage down the back of the throat (postnasal drainage),
	nasal obstruction or congestion, causing difficulty breathing through your nose, pain, tenderness and swelling around
	your eyes, cheeks, nose or forehead, reduced sense of smell and taste
    Causes
	Nasal polyps, deviated nasal septum, other diseases (HIV, immune system-related diseases, etc.), respiratory tract
	infections, and allergies such as hay fever.
    Risk factors
	A deviated septum, nasal polyps, asthma, aspirin sensitivity, a dental infection, an immune system disorder such as
	HIV/AIDS or cystic fibrosis, hay fever or another allergic condition, and regular exposure to pollutants such as 
	cigarette smoke
