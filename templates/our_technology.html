PRISTINE

Predicting your susceptibility to infectious diseases

     PRISTINE Technology
        The Problem - Everyone is genetically different, thus, to no surprise individuals
        can respond differently to infectious diseases. Currently, there is a lack of
        understanding of how infectious diseases will impact an individual based on
        their genetic information.
        The objective  - To predict an individual's susceptibility to a wide range
        of infectious diseases based on their genetic information. 
	The biology  - There are a number of factors that play a vital role in ones
	susceptibility to infectious diseases. More specifically, the genetics of the
	host (us!) can determine ones susceptibility to infectious. Case-control studies
	that assess the association between genetic loci and infectious disease susceptibility
	are on-the-rise. These studies help identify possible candidate genes associated with
	infectious disease susceptibility. Our technology implements the use of two resources
        that have information on genetic variants attributed with susceptibility to specific
        infectious diseases like COVID-19, the chicken pox, measles, and more! Reference.
	In addition, blood type has been investigated to play a role in COVID-19 susceptibility
	and outcome. For example, there have been reports of a protective effect in blood
	group O and a higher risk in blood group A for severe covid-19 progression.Reference.
        Don't have your genetic information ready to input? No need to worry. You can
        get your genotype file from various personal genomics companies like 23andMe,
        FamilyTreeDNA, and Ancestry. 
        The calculation - We will calculate a PRS score for each disease, using
        the 23andMe summary statistics for all of the infectious diseases except COVID-19.
	The PRS score for COVID-19 is calculated using the COVID-19 host genetics initiative results,
	In addition, the blood group associated with an individual's genotype will be assessed and
	reported in the COVID-19 susceptibility report. The user's genetic informaion will be converted
	into percentiles for each inectious disease depending where on the PRS distribution they fall
	for their particular ancestry.
        What is a polygenic risk score (PRS)? A PRS is an estimate of an individual's
        genetic liability to a trait/phenotype/disease, calculated according to their genotype
        profile and relevant genome-wide association study (GWAS) data.
        DISCLAIMER
        The outputs of this pipeline are BIOINFORMATIC INFERENCES and are not experimentally validated. These results do not confirm
        that you will have increased or decreased susceptibility but rather provide insight of the possibility. Please consult health
        professionals regarding your susceptibility to different pathogens.
