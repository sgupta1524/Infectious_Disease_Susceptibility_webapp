	About the results:
	Our results are presented in percentiles. A percentile is a number where a certain percentage
	of scores fall below that number. Therefore if you are at a 50th percentile for a given infectious
	disease, out of 100 normal people of your ancestry 50 will have a lower susceptibility score and
	50 will have a higher susceptibility score. The susceptibility score is a genetic calculation that
	weighs a personâ€™s odds of contracting each infectious disease.
	These percentiles are generated using SNPs from a 23andMe study that investigated the susceptibility
	of individuals to a series of infectious diseases.
